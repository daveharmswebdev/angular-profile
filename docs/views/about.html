Backstory

That program spit out a text file that got loaded into payroll program and it fooled the owners into thinking I knew something about computers. I spent the next five years being a jack of all trades, networking, hardware, software, tech support, etc. Tech support was my favorite, I loved helping people. That inspired me into getting into nursing.

Nursing has been good to me. And I want very badly to be good to nursing. And I think I can do that through IT. Nurses need help; they need better IT. Healthcare needs more nurses involved in software projects. And that’s where I want to be.

I know the workflow on the floor. I know the human, physical story behind assessment, medication, and treatment. I can look at the data and see a face.

Nashville

When I was a student at nursing school in Richmond I would visit my parents in Thompson Station, which is about 30 minutes south on 65. And I just loved it here. I moved after graduation. That was in 2009. And life has been great. I’ve had an excellent career as a nurse. I met my wife here, started a family. I love the weather here, it’s never as cold as Massachusetts, where I was raised. And it’s never as hot as Texas, where I went to college. I have rental property in East Nashville, in-laws in Clarksville, and my parents in the south. So I’m pretty rooted here.
